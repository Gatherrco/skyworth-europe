Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Skyworth Europe uses cookies and similar technologies to ensure the proper functioning of our website, analyze navigation, and display multimedia content.

Last updated: 7 January 2026

1) What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, such as your preferred language or whether you have accepted cookies.

2) How we use cookies

We use our own and third-party cookies to:

  • Make the website technically accessible and usable (core functionality).
  • Measure and improve performance and user-friendliness (analytics/statistics).
  • Display multimedia content and enable certain embedded features (where applicable).

Where required by law, we will ask for your consent before placing non-essential cookies on your device.

3) Types of cookies we may use

A) Technically Required (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are required for the website to work and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in (if applicable), or filling in forms.

  • Navigation and basic page functions
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Cookie consent preferences
  • Load balancing / performance stability

B) Analysis & Statistics

We use analytics technologies (including cookies) to understand how visitors use the website (for example, which pages are visited most, how users move through the site, and whether errors occur). This helps us improve the website and its performance.

These cookies may collect information such as IP-derived approximate location, device/browser type, pages visited, and time spent on pages. Where required, we only use these cookies if you consent.

C) Functional (Preferences) — if enabled

Functional cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as language or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. If these cookies are disabled, some features may not work properly.

D) Marketing / Advertising — if enabled

Marketing cookies may be set through our site by us or our advertising partners (if used). They may be used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant messaging on other websites. We will only use these cookies where applicable and where you provide consent (where required).

4) Third-party cookies

Some cookies may be placed by third parties that provide services on our website (for example, analytics providers or embedded media). These third parties may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites. Their use of cookies is governed by their own privacy and cookie policies.

5) Managing your cookie choices

You can manage cookies in two ways:

  • Cookie banner/settings (if available): you can accept all, reject all, or customize your preferences.
  • Browser controls: most browsers let you block or delete cookies. Note: blocking some cookies may impact site functionality.

6) How long cookies stay on your device

Cookies may be session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent cookies (remain until they expire or you delete them). The duration depends on the cookie’s purpose and configuration.

7) Updates to this Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes to cookies we use, legal requirements, or operational reasons. Any updates will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date.
